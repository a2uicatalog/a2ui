NASA's crewed return to the Moon, phased: uncrewed and crewed test flights first, orbital rendezvous demonstration next, lunar landing after. The infrastructure rows (SLS, Orion, commercial landers) are prerequisites for the missions the public actually watches.

# Phases

## 1 · Foundation flights {.status-shipped}

Certification of the launch and crew vehicles — the plumbing of the programme.

1. Artemis I — uncrewed lunar flyby
   status: done
   below: SLS + Orion integrated flight test, uncrewed
   above: first SLS/Orion flight around the Moon (Nov–Dec 2022)
   unlocks: crew certification for Artemis II
2. Artemis II — crewed lunar flyby
   status: done
   below: crewed life support and high-speed reentry validated over a 694,481-mile journey
   above: first crewed lunar flyby in over 50 years — launched 1 April 2026, splashdown off San Diego 10 April
   unlocks: rendezvous and docking demonstration
   note: crew — Wiseman, Glover, Koch (NASA), Hansen (CSA)

## 2 · Orbital rendezvous era {.status-active}

Artemis III was redefined: a crewed demonstration flight in low Earth orbit rather than a lunar landing.

1. Artemis III — LEO rendezvous and docking demo
   status: in-progress
   below: Orion rendezvous and docking with commercial landers from Blue Origin and SpaceX
   above: crewed demo flight in Earth orbit, scheduled late 2027
   unlocks: crewed lunar landing missions
   note: crew announced 9 June 2026 — Bresnik, Parmitano, Rubio, Douglas
2. Post-flight assessments of Artemis II
   status: in-progress
   below: initial assessments feeding vehicle and mission design forward
   unlocks: Artemis III mission definition

## 3 · Lunar landing {.status-planned}

1. Crewed lunar landing
   status: planned
   note: deferred beyond Artemis III in the June 2026 replan; depends on commercial lander readiness

# Timeline

- late 2027 :: Artemis III :: crewed LEO rendezvous/docking demo with commercial landers
